Pattern: Bento Grid Use case: Product Discovery

Layout intent

Bento grids let shoppers scan and compare without scrolling endlessly.

The bento pattern arranges content in modular compartments of varying sizes. Featured items claim more real estate while supporting products remain visible. This creates natural visual hierarchy without hiding inventory behind tabs or carousels.

Research shows shoppers compare 2.4x more products when using grid layouts versus single-column feeds. The pattern works especially well for curated collections and seasonal drops.

Ideal for

Product catalogs, seasonal collections, and curated bundles.

Key benefit

Reduces decision fatigue while maintaining discovery.

Grid structure

Use a 12-column grid with gap-4 for tight layouts or gap-6 for breathing room. Mix column spans to create visual hierarchy.

.grid {
  display: grid;
  grid-template-columns:
    repeat(12, 1fr);
  gap: 1rem;
}

Card patterns

Every product card should show: image, category, title, price, and rating. Stock indicators build urgency. Keep CTAs consistent.

In stock — neutral, reassuring
Low stock — creates urgency
Last items — high urgency

Performance tips

Use aspect-ratio to reserve space and prevent layout shift. Lazy load images below the fold. Keep hero images under 200KB.

Target CLS < 0.1
Target LCP < 2.5s
