Use case: Launch Pattern: Hero

Launch hero

Launch in one screen: clear headline, single CTA, no clutter.

Hero layouts work when the first fold answers three questions: what is it, who is it for, and why now. Research shows 57% of viewing time happens above the fold, so the hero must feel complete without hiding the action below.

Keep the headline to 5–6 words, show a product mockup or story image, and pair it with one primary action. Use a secondary link only if it supports conversion (demo, pricing, or case study).

Proof in the second fold

Simplified layouts outperform complex ones.

A/B testing shows that simplified pages lift conversions. A Ubisoft checkout redesign moved conversion from 38% to 50%. Another product page redesign increased revenue per visitor by 17.1%. Hero layouts should carry a short proof block immediately after the hero to reinforce the value proposition.

Hero checklist

Headline answers what it is and who it’s for.

Single primary CTA with a supporting action if needed.

Hero media has a fixed aspect ratio to prevent CLS.

Navigation stays minimal to avoid distraction.

Final CTA

Keep the last screen short and decisive.

Hero-driven pages should end with a single decision. Avoid stacking multiple offers. Make the CTA feel like the natural next step after the story.
